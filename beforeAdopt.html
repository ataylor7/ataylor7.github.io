Is Your House Ready? Are You?

It is important to remember that a cat is a living creature. They have needs, too! Before getting a cat, you should make sure you and your house are prepared. Make sure you have these before the cat sets foot in your home. A good starter list is:

  • 2 litterboxes: you should get 1 more than number of cats you own, as long as you have space
  • Food and food bowls. Choose bowls that are easy to clean and won't mold. Choose food carefully; you can consult a vet about diet if needed.
  • Scratching posts! This will save your furniture.
  • Plenty of toys! Cats like to play too!
  • Treats!
Another thing to consider is breed. Most shelter cats will be domestic short-hairs, but many can be mixed, so knowing about characteristics can be helpful. For example, Siamese cats tend to be very vocal. Watch out for Siamese features if you want a quiet home life! Get a list of breeds below.
